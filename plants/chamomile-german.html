German Chamomile

Botanical Name

Matricaria chamomilla

Body Systems Affected

Digestive System Nervous System Skin System

Common Names

English: Chamomile

Danish: Kamille

French: Camomille

Habitat and Growing Conditions

Habitat

Natural habitat: Prefers sunny locations on roadsides, wastelands, and fields. It is a weed species that grows well in various climates across Europe, Asia, and North America.

Soil preference: Thrives in sandy or loamy, well-drained soils but can tolerate poor conditions and moderately saline soils.

Sunlight: Full sun is ideal, but it can tolerate some light shade.

Growing Conditions

Temperature: Prefers daytime temperatures of 19-25°C but can tolerate a wider range. Young plants are susceptible to damage from frost (0°C), while mature plants can survive dormancy down to about -5°C.

Soil pH: Best in a pH range of 5.5-6.5, but tolerates 5.0-7.0.

Watering: Water regularly to keep the soil moist, especially since the roots are shallow. Avoid waterlogging.

Fertilizer: Does not require much fertilizer, as it grows well in poor soils. Over-fertilizing can reduce oil yield.

Maintenance: Prune or deadhead regularly to encourage new growth and more flowers. After the first harvest, trim the stems to promote new development.
