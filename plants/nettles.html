Nettles

Botanical Name

Urtica dioica

Body Systems Affected

Digestive System Woman Reproductive System Male Reproductive System

Common Names

English: Nettles

Danish: Brændenælde

French: Ortie

Habitat and Growing Conditions

Habitat

Natural habitat: Found abundantly in temperate regions throughout Europe, Asia, North America, and North Africa.

Preferred locations: Thrives in disturbed soils, along roadsides, woodland edges, riverbanks, hedgerows, and waste ground.

Soil preference: Rich, moist, nitrogen-rich soils with high organic matter content. Often indicates fertile soil.

Sunlight: Prefers partial shade but tolerates full sun if soil remains moist.

Moisture: Requires consistently moist soil; commonly found near water sources.

Growing Conditions

Perennial: Hardy perennial that spreads via creeping rhizomes and can form dense colonies.

Hardiness: Very hardy, survives in USDA zones 3-10. Tolerates temperatures down to -40°C.

Soil pH: Prefers slightly acidic to neutral pH (6.0-7.5).

Water: Keep soil consistently moist but well-drained. Established plants are drought-tolerant but quality diminishes.

Propagation: Can be grown from seed in early spring or by dividing rhizomes in spring or autumn.

Growth: Reaches 1-2 meters (3-6 feet) in height. Dies back in winter and re-emerges in spring.

Caution: Spreads vigorously and can become invasive. Plant in contained areas or manage carefully.

Active Constituents

Leaves: Flavonoids, phenolic compounds, organic acids, vitamins, mineral salts, protein, boron, vitamins A, C, D, E, F, K, P. Proteins represent 30% of the dry mass. Mineral substances content is approximately 20% of the dry mass.

Root: Lectins, polysaccharides, sterols, lignans.

Fruits (seeds): Saturated and unsaturated fatty acids, carotenoids (β-carotene, lutein, violaxanthin), vitamins.

Properties

Antiallergic, Anti-inflammatory (topical), Antilithic, Antirheumatic, Blood purifying, Hemostatic, Blood building, Choleretic, Uterine contractor, Galactagogue, Prostate growth inhibiting

Side Effects

Do not take in large quantities if you have kidney failure

Do not take if you suffer from hemochromatosis (excess iron)

Nettle is rich in vitamin K, therefore it is contraindicated if you are taking vitamin K antagonist anticoagulants (such as warfarin)
